Who are we?

We are a group of seven students from Aalborg University. MCGA which is focused on grid computing, aims to create an efficient, secure, and incentivized platform that allows users to share their CPU power in a controlled and rewarding way. Through MCGA, we hope to empower individuals to contribute to large-scale computing efforts while maintaining full control over their resources.

How does MCGA work?

MCGA is an extension that allows users to contribute their CPU power to a distributed computing network. The extension runs in the background, so you won't experience any disruption to your regular activities. In return for your contribution, you receive rewards based on the amount of power you share. The system tracks your usage transparently, so you can see how your resources are being used and monitor the rewards you earn. MCGA is designed to be secure, flexible, and rewarding, making it easy and beneficial for users to take part in the grid computing network.

Security of Data

The team behind MCGA understands the importance of trust in a collaborative computing environment. Our platform offers users complete control over how much CPU power they contribute, ensuring flexibility and transparency in their involvement. By rewarding users for their contributions with clear and fair incentives, a system that values both effort and trust is created. Security measures are taken to protect users' systems and data, while our transparent tracking system allows users to see exactly how their resources are being utilized. This combination of autonomy, reward, and security makes our platform a reliable choice for users who want to participate in grid computing while maintaining control over their resources.
